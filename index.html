About TED

TED is a nonprofit organization devoted to Ideas Worth Spreading. Started as a four-day conference in California 30 years ago, TED has grown to support its mission with multiple initiatives. The two annual TED Conferences invite the world's leading thinkers and doers to speak for 18 minutes or less. Many of these talks are then made available, free, at TED.com.

An Idea

TED is a non-profit organization devoted to Ideas Worth Spreading. It started out in 1984 as a conference bringing together people from three worlds- Technology, Entertainment and Design.

Success

Since then its scope has become ever broader. Along with two annual conferences - the TED Conference in Long Beach and Palm Springs each spring, and the TED Global Conference in Edinburgh UK each summer.

Award Winner

TED includes the award-winning TED Talks video site, the Open Translation Project and TED Conversations, the inspiring TED Fellows and TEDx programs, and the annual TED Prize.

About TEDx

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TEDTalks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized. (Subject to certain rules and regulations.)

Theme: ALCHEMY OF SHARDS

Alchemy of Shards highlights the property of bringing together the right pieces to build something stronger and better
“Bringing together just the right pieces”

Alchemy- a seemingly magical process of transformation, creation, or combination. Usually, the transformation of ordinary metals into gold
Shards- pieces
“When something breaks, if the pieces are large enough, you can fix it. Unfortunately, sometimes things do not break, they shatter. But when you let the light in, the shattered glass will glitter. And in those moments, when the pieces of what we caught the sun, I will remember just how beautiful it was.” This theme is based on the concept of Kintsugi. It is the 400-year-old Japanese art of putting broken pottery pieces back together with gold. It is built on the idea of embracing imperfections or failure. It highlights the broken pieces as a part of its design. What otherwise would have been considered as waste is now turned into something beautiful with the very "broken pieces" as its main selling point. Another way of looking at it is: when glass shatters, more light percolates through it again symbolizing accepting change which could be undesirable but we must be able to look at it from a perspective such that one can make the most out of it. Failure is but a stepping stone to success.
